When Girls Thrive, We All Thrive

 

Our Mission

 

WISER empowers girls and communities through transformative education and (w)holistic health.

Our Community

 

WISER is possible thanks to collaboration with the community of Muhuru Bay, Kenya, where we have worked for over 10 years.

 

ALWAYS GROWING

WISER is a community effort to open doors for girls through holistic approaches. After a decade, we have touched the lives of tens of thousands of people through education and health programs and we still strive for more. 
Our WISER alumni, like Nimon, are the ultimate testimony to the longevity of WISER's impact - not only in Muhuru Bay, but across Kenya and the world. 

The WISER team is made up of a dedicated group representing both our Kenyan entity, the WISER NGO, and our United States 501c3 nonprofit, WISER International. Together, we form a cohesive group striving for collective impact.

DR. SHERRYL BROVERMAN
Board Chair
SHERRYL BROVERMAN - BOARD CHAIR
Sherryl is a social entrepreneur and leader in science education reform, particularly for historically excluded groups. She is the Founder of both the WISER NGO and WISER Intl, is Chair Emeritus of WISER International and is the current Chair of the NGO. Sherryl is also a professor at Duke University in the Biology Department and the Duke Global Health Institute. Sherryl is a Senior Fellow with the National Center for Science and Civic Engagement, an international community to improve STEM education and civic engagement. Sherryl’s commitment to science education and civic engagement has been recognized by Duke University with the David and Janet von Brooks Award for Distinguished Teaching in the Sciences, the Dean’s Award for Diversity, and the Algernon Sydney Sullivan Award for excellence of character and service to humanity. She has also received the William E. Bennett Award for Extraordinary Contributions to Civic Engagement from the National Center for Science and Civic Engagement.

EILEEN

Eileen Vergino is passionate about seeing more women in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM), so she fit right in at WISER when she visited in 2017. What most impressed her was how committed students were not only to pursuing big, global dreams, but also, with transforming their local context. She loves how WISER creates role models so that young women in Muhuru Bay can pursue their dreams without limits.

Quote: “When I was at WISER, I loved spending time with the girls as they thought about problems, brainstormed solutions, and thought about how they could change their communities for the better. They’re not me-centered. They’re centered on ‘we’ and ‘us.’ That’s incredibly powerful.”
